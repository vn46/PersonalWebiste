Research: EmotionTrak (SciFi Lab)

A research project aims to detect humans' emotions on a daily basis through technology.

Kotlin, Python
×

Overview

Starting in the spring of my freshman year, I participated in SciFi Lab, a research group at Cornell University focusing on HCI. I'm mainly working with the EmotionTrak team of the lab. We used various methods and technology to track and study humans' emotions and behaviors on a daily basis. As research asssistant, my main goal is to test all the wearable devices built by graduate students, develop mobile apps to assist with collecting groundtruth of each device, and help with user studies.

What I've Learned

The most important thing I've learned is how to be a good researcher. I work on my own most of the time and there is noone constantly remind me to complete my task. But, I slowly learn to manage my time and to be a more proactive undergraduate assistant. I read a lot of papers about human emotions, have a chance to test all the wearable devices built by our graduate students, and explore more advanced CS fields like Machine Learning and Computer Vision. In the future, I want to learn more about Machine Learning through the project and be even more involved with the lab.
